Project Idea

My project - a fitness tracking collar for cats

My idea for an IT project is a fitness monitor for cats (it could also work for dogs or other pets), which would come in the form of some kind of collar with step tracking features. It would then sync with an app that would provide information to cat owners on how much exercise their cat has (or hasn’t) gotten over the course of a day. This information would be automatically organized into graphs and tables that can show day-by-day comparison of how much the cat has moved around, and whether they are meeting recommended amounts of exercise every day.

It is a well known fact that cats spend a lot of their time being lazy, with many cats spending the majority of their day either sleeping or lounging around. This is becoming an increasing problem as many cat owners now keep exclusively indoor cats. But with a surge of indoor cats, comes a surge of laziness, and cats not getting as much exercise as they need. This fitness monitor will allow owners the opportunity to find out whether their cat is exercise-deprived, and with this knowledge, owners, particularly owners of indoor cats, can take measures to increase their cat’s exercise, such as giving it new toys to play with.

The product comes with both hardware and software elements. The hardware is a collar to be worn by cats. It might function and look like similar fitness wearables for humans, such as Fitbits. It would function as a pedometer, that measures the steps a cat takes through motion, and possibly a basic GPS to measure distance travelled over the course of a day (this may also provide the handy feature of being able to locate a cat if it goes missing). This information would then be transmitted to the software side of the product; the companion app, available on mobile devices, or, users can sign in to the website through a browser. Owners can then access the data, and see how much exercise their cat is getting, in terms of steps taken every day. This data would be neatly organized intro graphs and tables so owners can compare the information across days, weeks, or months, and see if there is a trend in their cat’s exercise. Owners can set daily goals for their cats to meet, or use suggested goals based on how much exercise a cat is recommended to get every day. The app could also provide suggestions on how to get cats exercising more, differing depending on whether the cat is an indoor cat or outdoor cat (the blame isn’t entirely on the cat, it’s also the owners obligation to get their cat exercising!).

If GPS functionality was included, it could even show a map of where your cat has travelled, and even track their location at any time – obviously this feature would be more targeted towards outdoor cat owners than indoor cat owners. Another potential feature could be social integration – users could add fellow cat owners and compare statistics between them. This may actually motivate cat owners to get their cats exercising even more, if they see their friend’s cats exercising harder than their own.

However, the ultimate endgame of this product is super top secret. Hidden in the terms of service, users will agree to have their cat’s data monitored and collected. The data collected will be on the behavior of cats, and can then be sold (at a very high price) to companies in the pet industry, such as creators of cat food, and cat toys. They will then analyse this data and use it for their own nefarious deeds, as I earn money both from users and mega-companies in the pet industry!

The software elements of my product would need to be programmed, in a language such as Java, and the website could be created using HTML. The collar itself would send it’s data online, to the owner’s account, so the information could be accessed through the app or website. The collar’s hardware should be as minimal as possible, just sending the basic information such as steps/distance moved, and location, and the analysis and organization of the data can be handled online. The design of the collar would ideally need to be fairly lightweight, but still including a pedometer, potentially a GPS if technologically feasible, and a way to transmit this data online. When this product starts to get some attention, maybe I can collaborate with Fitbit for some design tips!

The skills required to create this product would include programming expertise, to create the app and website. Java could be used to write the app, considering I have no experience in any other programming language, as well as HTML for the website, considering I now have used a very small amount of HTML thanks to this assignment. I don’t think the programming requirements would be too farfetched, especially if working with a team. However, one issue that may arise would be hosting of the servers, website, account system, and all the incoming data that needs to be analysed server side, although I’m sure once our Kickstarter campaign makes triple its funding goal that shouldn’t be a problem at all. The hardware side of things is something I’m less knowledgeable about. Cheap options such as Raspberry Pi’s may not actually be feasible to fit onto a pet collar. To work properly, the technology would probably have to be similar to other fitness watches like the Fitbit, as they are fairly small and lightweight.

Overall, if this product were successful, it could potentially reduce overweightness in cats, and get cats across the world being more active. Indoor-only cats could be leading happier and more active lives if their owner’s are making the effort to monitor them and keep them active accordingly. For the product to succeed in this way it does also require owners to take some initiative to get their cats exercising. And of course, if the product were financially successful, it would have the wonderful outcome of making me lots of money!
